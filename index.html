Step Zero
Three Writing Systems
You Must Know
Phonetic · 46 chars
Hiragana
ひらがな
The foundation of Japanese. Used for native words, grammatical particles, verb endings. Every beginner starts here. Soft, curved shapes.
1–2 weeks
Phonetic · 46 chars
Katakana
カタカナ
Used for foreign loanwords, technical terms, onomatopoeia, emphasis. Same sounds as Hiragana but angular shapes. Learn right after Hiragana.
1–2 weeks
Logographic · 2136 chars
Kanji
漢字
Chinese-origin characters representing meaning + sound. The Jōyō set has 2,136 characters. Learn gradually over months/years alongside vocabulary.
1–3 years

書

The gold standard university textbook series. Structured, clear grammar explanations, workbook exercises. Best starting point.

Anki
Free
Spaced Repetition System flashcard app. Download the Core 2k/6k vocabulary deck. Non-negotiable for vocabulary retention.

WaniKani
Freemium · $9/mo
Structured Kanji + vocab SRS. Teaches radicals → kanji → vocabulary progressively. Free up to level 3. Very effective for Kanji.

Free
The best Japanese-English dictionary online. Search by word, kanji, reading, or draw kanji. Use this every single day.
 ↗

Free · YouTube
Input-focused YouTube channel. Videos at beginner, elementary, intermediate levels. Best immersion resource for early learners.
 ↗

iTalki
Freemium · ~$10–20/hr
Find professional Japanese tutors or community partners. Even 1 session/week dramatically improves speaking. Essential for output practice.

Free
Real Japanese news rewritten in simple Japanese with furigana. Perfect for N4–N3 reading practice with real-world vocabulary.

Study Strategies
Tips That Actually Matter
継
Consistency Over Intensity
30 minutes daily beats 4 hours once a week. Language learning is about building neural pathways, which requires repeated small exposures over time.
浸
Immersion from Day One
Start listening to Japanese immediately — even if you understand nothing. Your brain starts tuning into the sounds and rhythm of the language.
復
Use SRS Religiously
Anki reviews take 10–15 minutes daily. Do them every single day without fail. Missing days causes exponential review pile-up.
禁
Don't Use Romaji
Never use Romanized Japanese after the first week. It creates a crutch that slows your kana recognition for months. Learn hiragana fully first.
興
Mine Your Interests
Learn vocabulary from anime, games, or topics you already love. Context-rich learning sticks 3x better than word lists. Find your genre.
話
Speak Early, Speak Often
Many learners delay speaking for months. Start output practice at month 3–4, even if you make mistakes. Errors are essential feedback.
時
Track Your Hours
JLPT N3 requires roughly 450–600 study hours. N2 needs 600–1000. Tracking hours keeps you honest about your actual progress.
迷
Embrace Being Lost
When you read or listen and understand only 30%, that's fine. Comprehensible input theory says you learn best at i+1 — just above your level.
